The TA

The Product

As part of my schooling, I participated in the University of Calgary's Integrated Learning Stream (ILS) for second year electrical engineers. In one of our ENEL 300 courses, we created a product called the TA (Temperature Alarm). The alarm alerts plant owners if the temperature of the area or moisture of the soil is unsuitable.

The TA uses a PIC microcontroller as the brain of the product. The output of a voltage divider circuit with a thermistor in it is taken as input into one of the pins of the microcontroller. Similarly, the microcontroller reads the voltage from a moisture detector.

The microcontroller determines the temperature and moisture, which are both displayed to the screen via an LCD screen. If the temperature falls out of a range specified by the user, a signal is transmitted wirelessly to the alarm unit, alerting the user. For the moisture, the alarm will go off when the soil becomes too dry.

Our final product started off as a simple temperature alarm. We changed the idea to become wireless for convenience, and added a moisture sensor to gear it towards plant owners. Our reasoning was that the multifunctionality would be attractive to consumers and that there was a bigger potential market with the use for plants. There were contingency plans for the product, which was to develop an app to connect the temperature sensor to your phone.

Using an iterative method made it easy for us to modify the product. Following the SCRUM framework, we divided the work up into sprints. The team would meet often for updates and review our product at the end of every sprint to make the appropriate changes.

Teamwork

The product we developed didn’t come without challenges. Working with a team and implementing the SCRUM framework for the first time had some rough patches. It was difficult for us to take initiative and develop ideas that were feasible for us to pull off considering the pandemic.

The most difficult aspect of the project was working on a technical product remotely. It was hard for us to work on a product when we didn’t have the physical product to look at together. We overcame this meeting frequently online and by focusing on the software aspect of the product.

What I found especially rewarding was how our team was able to get together to push out a design for a product despite working virtually. The ability to communicate virtually with others is an asset that will continue to help me move forward. I'm proud of our team for being resourceful with what we had; it was truly making lemonade out of lemons.
