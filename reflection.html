Reflection

Another attribute that’s helped me has been taking the time to reflect upon myself. I’m able to grow from my past experiences by continuing to do what went right and fixing what went wrong.

Learning Strategies

As an example, in my ENEL 300 course, I was asked to reflect on my learning for the semester. Upon reflection, I found that the following things were beneficial to my learning, so I kept them in my routine:

  • Breaking up study sessions into smaller bits
  • Integrating breaks into my schedule

On the other hand, I found these things were not benefiting me, so I modified them or cut them out of my routine:

  • Rather than rushing to get more work done, I should take more time to absorb the concepts better (quality > quantity)
  • Spending too much time on problems I can’t figure out
  • Seeking more help from my peers when I’m stuck

The ability to reflect on my learning strategies is only one example of how reflection has allowed me to become a flexible person that is able to learn from past perspectives. This concept not only applies to my personal life but also to the work I do.
