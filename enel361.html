ENEL 361

Electronic Devices and Materials

What is ENEL 361?

In ENEL 361, I learned about electronic devices, specifically those that use semiconductors such as diodes, light-emitting diodes, photodiodes, and transistors. Another thing I learned was how to analyze circuits with these semiconductor components in them.

I liked this course because I appreciated learning about the materials that make up our everyday products like phones, laptops, and much more; it's amazing to finally know about the physics of charge carriers and how they move in pn junctions, which is how all of silicon chips that are in our devices work.

Hands on Activities

As part of the course, we're asked to build our own circuits to test the electronic devices that are talked about in class. Examples of practical circuits I built are a bridge rectifier and a voltage regulator.

Getting hands-on experience with following schematics to build real, physical circuits, was a valuable thing I learned. In addition, the labs allowed me to develop my bench skills with the oscilloscope such as:

  • Reading measurements like voltage, frequency, and period
  • Graphing IV characteristics using XY-mode
  • Triggering signals
  • Generating signals
