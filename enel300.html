ENEL 300

Electrical and Computer Engineering Professional Skills

Design Process

We learned about the software development cycle and project management in ENEL 300. If I had one takeaway from the class, it’d be that I gained valuable leadership and teamwork skills. Through the process of developing a product with my team, I learned about just how much communication a team needs to be successful.

We also learned about the software development cycle using the SCRUM framework. We broke our project down into short sprints, which gave us much more flexibility. Due to the pandemic we had to change things as we went multiple times; the SCRUM framework made this easy to execute.

A difficult aspect of this course was communicating with others in a way where they understand what exactly you’re talking about. I found that my teammates sometimes didn’t completely get what I was trying to express.

Moving forward from these experiences I'm now able to more clearly articulate my thoughts and find mediums to do so whether it be verbally, visually, or by writing/typing. I can among other things:

  • Create clear task boards.
  • Write user stories for a product.
  • Keep track of work progress using methods such as burndown charts.
  • Articulate my thoughts well at meetings.

Entrepreneurship

ENEL 300 taught me about entrepreneurship too. I’ve gained the skills to make an effective product targeted at a specific user group in order to make the most profit. Some of these include:

  • Making pitches
  • Marketing a product
  • Looking at user personas
  • Making value propositions
  • Making a business plan

I’ve gotten used to putting myself in the shoes of the end users to develop effective user stories to guide product development. I’ve learned to make sure to target the things that users are interested in and to consider what might be going on in their lives to relieve pain points they might have.

When giving pitches, I learned to deliver them with confidence, looking my audience in the eye as I describe the benefits of our product. It was a bit difficult to put myself out there and make pitches at first, but repeated presentations to our peers has allowed me to be more confident when talking about my products.

The thing that stuck with me the most in this class was to always consider what the end users might want. It’s imperative that I put myself in their situation and respond to their feedback.
